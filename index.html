The Clockmaker’s Child

They say a child was left at the library doors at midnight. The librarian took him in… but he never grew older. Now, anyone who passes by the giant bronze clock swears they hear laughter echoing inside the gears. Some believe the child is still trapped in time, waiting for someone to open the door and set him free.

The Pages That Bleed

A young woman wrote a forbidden romance but died before it was finished. Years later, the book returned—completed. The strange part? The ink was not ink. Whoever reads it to the final line feels the same curse as the author: a love that suffocates, a pain that crushes, and a heartbeat far too slow to be human.

The Room That Doesn’t Exist

Hidden between the shelves, there’s a small blue door. During the day: locked. At night: open and glowing. Everyone who entered found a different room— one filled with dusty toys, one with doors that led nowhere, and one, they say, contained a mirror that blinked. No one entered twice and remained the same.

The Man Who Drank Words

Every night, an old man stepped inside the library. He finished a book in a minute, and when he closed it, all the letters vanished from the pages. One night, he disappeared, leaving behind a single volume— empty, except for a handwritten message on the last page: “I’m full. Now it’s your turn.”

The Book That Whispers Names

You open it—nothing. Pages empty, untouched. But when you lean closer, a voice whispers your name, quiet and breathless. If you close the book too quickly, you may hear another voice from behind the door: — Come back… I haven’t written the ending yet…

The Eyeless Librarian

She walks silently through the aisles, never touching the ground. Even without eyes, she always finds the ones who shouldn’t be there. Readers say their blood freezes before they see her. Legend says she collects the eyes of those who return their books too late. If you feel someone staring at you… don’t look back.
